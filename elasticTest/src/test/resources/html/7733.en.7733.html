Amendments to Chapters

1) INF Code(Res. MSC.88(71)) was made mandatory under chapter VII of the Convention.
2) FSS Code(Res.98(73)) was mandatory under chapter II-2 of the Convention.
3) IMDG Code(Res.122(75)) was mandatory under chapter VII of the Convention.
4) Paragraph (a), (v) which was adopted on 18 May 2006 by Res.MSC.204(81) shall be beemed to have been accepted on the date on which it accepted by two-thirds of the Contracting Governments. It shall enter into force six months after the accepted date.
5) The existing regulations of Parts A, B, B-1, B-2, B-3 and B-4 of Chapter II-1 which were adopted by Annex 2 of Res.MSC.194(80) were re-adopted as Parts A, B, B-1, B-2, B-3 and B-4 by Annex 2 of Res.MSC.216(82) and it enter into force on 1 January 2009.
Of all the international conventions dealing with maritime safety, the most important is the SOLAS Convention. It is also one of the oldest, the first version having been adopted at a conference held in London in 1914. The incident which led to the convening of the 1914 Conference on Safety of Life at Sea was the sinking of the Titanic on her maiden voyage in 1912, when more than 1,500 passengers and crew died.
Since then there have been four further versions of SOLAS: the second was adopted in 1929 and entered into force in 1933, the third was adopted in 1948 and entered into force in 1952, the fourth was adopted (under the auspices of IMO) in 1960 and entered into force in 1965, the present version was adopted in 1974 and entered into force in 1980. SOLAS 1974 will not be replaced by a new Convention because it can be amended by the new procedure included in article VIII.
